Political Leadership
Sri Lanka—a land of rich history, culture, and leadership. Among its most influential leaders is Mahinda Rajapaksa, a man who shaped the country's modern political landscape.
Welcome to Sri Lanka
Located in the Indian Ocean, southwest of the Bay of Bengal, separated from the Indian peninsula by the Gulf of Mannar and the Palk Strait. It shares a maritime border with the Maldives in the southwest and India in the northwest. Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte is the legislative capital of Sri Lanka and the largest city, Colombo is the administrative and judicial capital which is the nation's political, financial and cultural centre.
Population
The current population of Sri Lanka is 23,188,536 as of Wednesday, March 5, 2025, based on Worldometer's elaboration of the latest United Nations data1. Sri Lanka 2025 population is estimated at 23,229,470 people at mid year. Sri Lanka population is equivalent to 0.28% of the total world population. Sri Lanka ranks number 60 in the list of countries (and dependencies) by population. The population density in Sri Lanka is 370 per Km2 (959 people per mi2). The total land area is 62,710 Km2 (24,212 sq. miles). 18.1 % of the population is urban (4,193,316 people in 2025). The median age in Sri Lanka is 33.3 years.
Languages and Dialects
Sinhala and Tamil are the official languages of Sri Lanka. Sinhala is widely spoken in the southern, western and central parts of the island, while Tamil is almost exclusively spoken in northern and eastern parts of the island.
Cultural Festivals
Every year on or about April 13 Sinhalese and Tamil people celebrate Sinhalese and Tamil New Year Festival, Muslims celebrate Mawlid, fast during the Islamic month Ramadan and celebrate at the end of the month with the festival which is (Eid al-Fitr) and (Eid al-Adha) is celebrated on the final month of the Islamic calendar known as Dhu al-Hijjah. Christians celebrate Easter and Christmas. Esala Perahera (A-suh-luh peh-ruh-ha-ruh) is a grand festival in the month of Esala held in Sri Lanka.[1] Happening in July or August in Kandy, it has become a unique symbol of Sri Lanka.
Local Entertainment
Sri Lanka is rightfully known as the "Wonder of Asia." Sri Lanka is a multicultural country which boasts of its unique nature as well as cultural heritage which enriches the richness of the country. The country boasts of various folk dances, today there are three distinct traditions of dances of Sri Lanka which differ from each other based on the costume, rhythm, and body movements.
Personality
Sri Lankans are typically characterized by their friendliness, humility, and a strong sense of community. Social customs in Sri Lanka emphasize respect for elders, hospitality, and a collective approach to life. The psychological makeup of Sri Lankans is deeply intertwined with their cultural identity, which values modesty, patience, and a calm demeanor. The concept of "serenity" is often highlighted in Sri Lankan culture, influenced by Buddhist teachings that advocate for inner peace and mindfulness. This cultural identity fosters a society where people are generally cooperative, empathetic, and supportive of one another.
Traditional Sports
Traditional sports Some of the traditional sports in Sri Lanka include Kabaddi, Gillidanda (proto-cricket), Kili thadthu, Kanna muchi, Chak-gudu, Thenkai addi and Elle. There are two styles of martial arts native to Sri Lanka, which are Cheenadi and Angampora. Sinhalese New Year sports Games such as Kotta Pora (Pillow fight), Ankeliya (tugging the horn), Kana mutti bindeema (breaking pots), Porapol Gaseema, Lissana gaha nageema (climbing the greasy pole), Banis Kaema (bun eating contest) and Gudu Keliya are played during Avurudu, the Sinhalese New Year.
Communication
PickMe is a Sri Lankan ride-hailing and delivery app, kind of like Uber but with some local twists. It lets you book tuk-tuks, cars, and vans for getting around, and it also has services for food delivery, parcel delivery, and even groceries. It’s super popular in cities like Colombo, Kandy, and Galle, where public transport can be a hassle, and hailing a tuk-tuk on the street might mean negotiating prices. With PickMe, the fares are set by the app, so there’s no need to haggle.
Environment
Sri Lanka has a rich and diverse environmentThe country has well-preserved rainforests like Sinharaja and numerous national parks such as Yala and Wilpattu, which protect wildlife., but it also faces several environmental challenges Illegal logging, agriculture, and urban expansion threaten forests and biodiversity.
Health and Wellness
Sri Lanka boasts health indicators that surpass regional averages, reflecting a relatively healthy population. As of 2018, life expectancy was 72.1 years for men and 78.5 years for women, ranking the country 70th globally. The Human Rights Measurement Initiative reports that Sri Lanka fulfills 86.7% of its expected right to health based on income levels
Asian Beauty
Sri Lankan beauty standards blend traditional and modern influences, valuing fair skin, long dark hair, big expressive eyes, and a naturally curvy yet slim figure. While historical preferences leaned toward lighter skin, there is growing appreciation for diverse tones. Traditionally, minimal makeup and natural beauty were emphasized, with coconut oil commonly used for hair care. Modern trends favor a fit and healthy look, individuality, and confidence, with both traditional attire like sarees and contemporary fashion being admired. Social media and globalization have influenced a shift towards embracing diversity and body positivity, making beauty standards more inclusive over time.
